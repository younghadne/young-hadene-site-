Legal Requirements

Business Registration, Contracts, and Compliance

Legal Framework Contracts Copyrights

Business Registration and Structure

Choose the right business structure for your record label – sole proprietorship, partnership, LLC, or corporation. Each has different legal implications, tax consequences, and liability protections. LLCs are popular for independent labels as they offer personal liability protection while maintaining tax flexibility.

Register your business name and obtain necessary licenses and permits. This includes business registration, tax identification numbers, and any music industry-specific licenses. Consider trademark protection for your label name and logo to prevent others from using similar branding.

Artist Contracts and Agreements

Develop comprehensive artist contracts that clearly define rights, responsibilities, and revenue sharing. Key elements include term length, territory rights, royalty rates, advance payments, and creative control provisions. Consider different deal structures – traditional record deals, distribution deals, or label services agreements.

Work with entertainment lawyers to draft contracts that protect both the label and artists. Include provisions for marketing commitments, delivery requirements, and dispute resolution. Clear contracts prevent misunderstandings and provide legal protection for all parties involved.

Copyright and Publishing Rights

Understand music copyright fundamentals – master rights (sound recording) and publishing rights (composition). As a label, you typically control master rights, while publishing may be controlled by songwriters or separate publishing companies.

Register your recordings with appropriate copyright offices and performance rights organizations (PROs) like ASCAP, BMI, or SOCAN. This ensures you receive royalties when your music is played publicly, streamed, or used in other media. Consider mechanical licensing for physical and digital distribution.

Distribution and Licensing Agreements

Carefully review distribution agreements with digital platforms and physical distributors. Understand the terms regarding territory, exclusivity, revenue splits, and ownership rights. Digital distributors typically take 15-30% commission, while physical distribution may involve different structures.

Licensing agreements for sync placements (film, TV, commercials) require special attention to rights and compensation. Work with music supervisors or licensing agencies to maximize these opportunities while protecting your artists' interests.

Compliance and Regulatory Requirements

Stay compliant with music industry regulations including royalty reporting, tax obligations, and labor laws. Understand your responsibilities for paying royalties to artists, songwriters, and producers. Keep detailed records of all transactions and payments.

Consider international requirements if you operate globally. Different countries have different copyright laws, collection societies, and tax regulations. Work with international partners or distributors who understand local requirements.
